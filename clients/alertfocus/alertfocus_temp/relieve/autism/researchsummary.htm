Autism and Neurofeedback Research Summary

 

EEG Neurofeedback study (Jerusiewicz, 2002)

Х         20 children with ASD in experimental group.     

Х         20 children with ASD in control group

Х         Experimental group completed an average of 36 neurofeedback sessions

Х      Pre- and post-treatment scores on the Autism Treatment Evaluation Checklist (ATEC) were collected.

Pilot study results: percentage of improvement on ATEC

                       Neurofeedback group        Control group

Speech/Language            29%                       0%

Sociability                         33%                       7%

Sensory/Cognitive            17%                       0%

Health/Physical/Behavior  26%                       5%

Total                                 26%                       3%

 

HEG Neurofeedback research (Limsila & Toomim, 2003)

Х         Largest HEG study to date.

Х         180 autistic children in Thailand

Х         After 40 sessions, the cohort's mean HEG readings (prefrontal activation) increased 53%

Х         Of the 81 subjects who were studying in public school, 86% increased their GPA by more than 0.5 (mean = 0.94) points on a 4-point scale

Х         Only 4% decreased their GPA by more than 0.5 points (mean = 0.57)

 

EEG Neurofeedback research (Coben & Padolsky, 2006)

Х         37 subjects with autism Х         20 EEG neurofeedback sessions

Х         Wait list control group (n=12) matched for gender, age, race, handedness, IQ & treatment

Х         89% success rate

Х         40% reduction of autistic symptoms on ATEC

Х         Significant improvements on neuropsychological tests of Attention, Language, Visual-Perception & Executive Functioning

Х         QEEG & infrared imaging demonstrated gains

 

HEG Neurofeedback Research (Coben 2006)

Х         28 subjects with autism

Х         20 nir or pir HEG sessions

Х         Wait list control group (n=12) matched for gender, age, race, handedness, IQ & treatment

Х         All subjects had previously completed 20 sessions of EEG neurofeedback

Х         All subjects had identified frontal system dysfunction based on neurobehavioral testing, neuropsychological testing, infrared imaging, and QEEG data

Х         90% success rate

Х         42% reduction in autistic symptoms on ATEC

Х         43% reduction in social interaction deficits

Х         47% reduction in communication deficits

Х         44% reduction in sensory/cognitive deficits

Х         39% reduction in behavior deficits

Х         Statistically significant (p < .05) improvements in neurobehavioral and neuropsychological functioning
