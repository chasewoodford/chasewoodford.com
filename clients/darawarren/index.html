I was born breech, and thus began a butt-first approach to life. I’m pretty sure Peter Griffin is my soul mate, or my Id (if my Id were a fat man) because there’s a good chance that, as you’re reading this, the word “shenanigans” would apply to whatever I’m doing.

As a young lass, I used to pretend to be a vampire. This was waaay before paranormal was en vogue, or goth even existed, so basically, I was a bookish weirdo to my peers… slash perky cheerleader. Because, you know, the outfits were cute, and it was gobs of fun.

I lived abroad twice, the first time in Budapest, on the Pest side (it’s pronounced Pesht, btw.) I was young and quasi- destitute, residing in communist-bloc housing. As such, if you want to ask me about the hoity-toity tourist stuff to do, I can’t help you. If you want to ask me about how I had to walk two miles to get fresh bread every day, trudging through hills of emission-blackened snow (there’s not a lot of plowing going on in Hungary), then I got stories for you. Actually, that’s the only story, and now you’ve heard it.

The second time, I lived abroad in merry ol’ England, applying for an independent work visa… but the British government deemed me “not an asset” and sent me back (true story). Before I left, I donned my old cheerleader uniform one more time, and waved my dark writings at the House of Parliament, shouting, “Not an asset? Not an asset? Have you seen my spirit-award-winning Hurkies? Did you read the story about the vindictive vegetables?” (Not entirely true story).

I currently reside in Philadelphia, toasting the birthplace of our liberty, snacking on hot soft pretzels (wit wiz), and walking the in the famed footsteps of the founding families and Will Smith and Kevin Bacon.

With my direwolf by my side (who’s way more Lady than Nymeria, though I try my hardest), we discover secret doorways to whimsical and enchanting lands. Because she was born without opposable thumbs, it’s up to me to pen the worlds for others to explore. I’ve written a collection for the curious, who’d like to find their way, or lose it.
